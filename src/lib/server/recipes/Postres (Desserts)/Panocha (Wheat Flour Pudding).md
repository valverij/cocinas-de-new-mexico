# Postres (Desserts)

## Panocha (Wheat Flour Pudding)

**Yield:** 20 servings  
**Temperature:** High, Medium-High, Low, 400°F  
**Baking Time:** Approximately 1 1/2 hours  
Freezes Well

### Ingredients

12 cups boiling water, approximately  
1 pound panocha flour  
2 cups flour  
1 1/2 cups sugar  
1/2 cup butter  
1 pound brown sugar  
1/4 teaspoon cloves (optional)  
1/4 teaspoon cinnamon (optional)

### Steps

1. Combine 5 cups boiling water, panocha flour, and flour in a large mixing bowl and mix until smooth. Cover bowl and set aside.

2. Place sugar in a heavy pan. Stir sugar at low heat until it is liquid and light, golden brown. Add remaining water to sugar and bring mixture to a boil at high heat.

3. Add butter, brown sugar, cloves, and cinnamon to sugar mixture and stir until sugar is dissolved.

4. Combine sugar and flour mixtures in a large, greased, oven-proof container. Boil mixture at medium-high heat for 15 minutes, stirring constantly.

5. Cover and bake panocha in a 400°F oven for approximately 1 1/2 hours, or until a pudding consistency has been reached. Chill before serving.
