# Platos Principales (Main Dishes)

## Posole (Hominy Stew)

**Yield:** 8-12 servings  
**Temperature:** High, Medium, Low  
**Cooking Time:** 6-7 hours[^1]  
**Freezes Well**

### Ingredients

1 pound prepared posole corn, thoroughly rinsed[^2]  
10 cups water  
1 pound pork or beef roast[^3]  
5 cups water, approximately  
2 tablespoons salt  
1 medium onion, chopped  
2 cloves garlic, minced  
1/4 teaspoon oregano  
1 teaspoon ground comino  
3-6 dried red chile pods, rinsed and crumbled[^4]

### Steps

1. Place posole and 10 cups water in large stewing pot. Bring mixture to a boil at high heat.

2. Reduce heat to low and simmer posole for 5 hours.

3. Approximately 1 hour before the completion of the simmering time, brown the pork in a large, heavy skillet on medium heat.

4. Add the pork to the stewing pot with 5 cups of water and continue to cook on low heat until tender.

5. Add the remaining ingredients to posole and simmer for an additional 1-2 hours. Adjust seasonings to suit taste.

[^1]: Posole may be cooked in a pressure cooker for 45 minutes at 15 pounds pressure.
[^2]: Posole corn is marketed dry or prepared.
[^3]: Varied amounts may be used.
[^4]: If desired, omit chile pods and serve with Red Chile Sauce or Green Chile Sauce.
