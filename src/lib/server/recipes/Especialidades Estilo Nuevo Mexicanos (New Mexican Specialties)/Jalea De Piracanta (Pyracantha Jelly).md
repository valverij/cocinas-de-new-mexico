# Especialidades Estilo Nuevo Mexicanos (New Mexican Specialties)

## Jalea de Piracanta (Pyracantha Jelly)

**Yield:** 4-5 half-pints  
**Temperature:** High, Medium-High, Low  
**Processing Time:** 5 minutes

### Ingredients

3 cups pyracantha berries[^1]  
6 cups water  
Juice of 1 lemon  
Juice of 1 grapefruit  
Cheesecloth  
1 1/2 ounces packaged, powdered pectin  
Sugar

### Steps

1. Wash and stem berries. Place berries and water in a kettle and simmer at low heat for 20 minutes.

2. Add lemon and grapefruit juice to berry mixture. Strain berry mixture through cheesecloth without squeezing berries.

3. Measure amount of liquid and discard berries.

4. Return liquid to kettle and add powdered pectin. Cook at high heat until mixture comes to a full boil. Reduce heat to medium-high.

5. Add the same amount of sugar as the measured liquid. Boil mixture at medium-high heat for 4-5 minutes, stirring constantly. (Mixture will be thin.)

6. Remove mixture from heat and skim off any foam that may have formed.

7. Pour mixture into hot, sterilized, half-pint canning jars. Seal jars according to the manufacturer's directions.

8. Process jars in a Boiling Water Bath for five minutes. Test seal when cooled.

[^1]: Pyracantha berries are the fruit of the pyracantha bush. The pyracantha bush is used as a colorful landscape accent in New Mexico.
